The Reality

The Shop
Doesn't Work
For You

"A diagnostic fee at a shop is $150. Mobile is $250. Most people don't know what they're actually paying for."

— Real numbers from the DFW area, 2025

The automotive industry runs on information asymmetry. You don't know what you don't know — and shops count on that. The result is recommendations you didn't need, services you already paid for, and a bill you can't argue with.

  • You get told you need service X and have no way to verify it
  • Maintenance schedules are designed to maximize shop visits, not your car's needs
  • Warning lights are designed to make you panic, not inform you
  • DIY YouTube videos exist, but no one to ask your specific question
  • The people who know the most charge the most to access them

FAQ

Common Questions

I know nothing about cars. Is this for me?
Yes — that's exactly who this is built for. You don't need any background. The whole point is to start from where you are and build real confidence with your specific vehicle. Nobody's going to make you feel dumb for asking basic questions.
What is Discord and do I need to know how to use it?
Discord is a free app — think of it like a group chat crossed with a forum. It's where the community lives, sessions happen, and resources are organized. It works on your phone, tablet, or computer. If you can use text messaging, you can use Discord. A short welcome guide walks you through it when you join.
Does this cover my specific car/bike?
The fundamentals — fluids, tires, brakes, warning lights, maintenance schedules — apply to almost every vehicle. For specific make/model questions, that's what the community channels and 1:1 sessions are for. You're not getting generic YouTube content. You get an actual tech who can talk about your situation.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. Discord memberships cancel anytime with no runaround. You keep access until the end of your billing period.
What's the difference between a group session and a 1:1?
Group sessions are open calls on Discord video — Kayce demos a concept, answers questions from the group, and covers general maintenance topics live. A 1:1 session is just you and Kayce for 45–60 minutes focused entirely on your vehicle, your situation, your questions. Both happen over video — no phone call required.
Is the $200 standalone session worth it without a membership?
A shop diagnostic in the DFW area runs $150 in-shop or $250 mobile. A 1:1 session here is $200, includes a full conversation about your situation, plain-English explanations, and a plan you can actually act on. If you end up joining a membership after, the math gets even better.
How do I pay? Do I need to pay inside the Discord app?
No — and you shouldn't. All memberships are purchased through a browser link, not inside the Discord app. This keeps the price the same regardless of whether you're on iPhone, Android, or desktop. You get a link in your email after joining that gives you your Discord role automatically.
